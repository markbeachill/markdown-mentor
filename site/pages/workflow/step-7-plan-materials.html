Step 7

Plan the materials

Who leads this step: AI, checked by you

Teaching and learning view

This is the main planning checkpoint. It protects learners from a pack that is too big, too thin, repetitive, or poorly matched to the learning goal.

WhatAsk the AI to produce a numbered inventory. Each item should have a title, material type, purpose, audience, sources, format, length, and special requirements.
WhyIt is much easier to fix a plan than to fix ten finished documents. The inventory lets you check coverage and balance before drafting starts.
NextEdit and approve the inventory, then generate one material at a time.

What to do

  1. Paste the Markdown library/content pack, brief, pedagogy, material specifications, and material list into the inventory prompt.
  2. Check that every planned material is useful and source-supported.
  3. Cut anything too broad, repetitive, or beyond the source pack.
  4. Approve the plan only when it is realistic.

Check before moving on

Ask: if these items were produced well, would they form a coherent teaching pack?

Technical appendix

This is the practical detail for running or finding the step in the repo.

Files and details

  • Useful prompt: Generate the materials inventory.
  • The inventory is a planning document, not a finished teaching material.
  • Each inventory item becomes one Markdown file later.
  • Keep the approved inventory somewhere safe so you can regenerate individual items later.
