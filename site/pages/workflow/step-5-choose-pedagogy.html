Step 5

Choose how to teach it

Who leads this step: You

Teaching and learning view

This step makes the learner experience explicit. The same topic feels very different for a primary pupil, an undergraduate, a vocational learner, or an ESL student.

WhatChoose a Pedagogy Specification from the library and edit it to fit your learners, context, tone, and support level.
WhyPedagogy tells the AI how to shape explanation, practice, feedback, pace, and challenge. It stops every output from sounding like the same generic lesson.
NextAfter choosing the teaching approach, choose the set of materials you want to make.

What to do

  1. Pick the closest library file rather than writing from scratch.
  2. Edit the learner level, tone, amount of support, and feedback style.
  3. Add any non-negotiables, such as short tasks, discussion first, retrieval practice, or graded language.
  4. Keep it reusable if you teach similar learners often.

Check before moving on

Ask: does this describe how I would actually teach these learners?

Technical appendix

This is the practical detail for running or finding the step in the repo.

Files and details

  • Pedagogy files live in library/pedagogy-specs.
  • Current examples include school-level, ESL/EFL, undergraduate, primary, and vocational.
  • The inventory prompt and material-generation prompt both use this file.
  • “Scaffolding” means the support learners get at first, which is reduced as they improve.
