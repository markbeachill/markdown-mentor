Prompts

Edit, then copy

WhatThe exact prompts for the AI steps in the workflow. Each one tells the AI what files it will get, what to produce, and what you should check.
WhyA clear task description gives better, more consistent results than a vague request. These keep the AI on shape.
NextEdit a prompt in its box if you wish, press Copy, then paste it into your AI chatbot. Paste your own files where the prompt asks.

How to use a prompt

Press Copy. Open your AI chatbot. Paste the prompt. Then paste your own content (your content pack, brief, and so on) where the prompt says [PASTE ... HERE]. The Copy button copies whatever is in the box, including your edits.

Suggest teaching briefs

AI gets
your Educational Content Training Pack (the Markdown file built by Markdown Mentor).
AI makes
two or three possible Teaching Briefs you could choose from.
You check
that each suggested brief is actually supported by the sources, and that the level and purpose match your learners.

Improve the content pack

AI gets
your content pack, your teaching goal, and (if you have it) the readiness note from Markdown Mentor.
AI makes
a short list of what is missing or weak, and what kind of source would fix each gap.
You check
that the suggestions are realistic for you to find or write, and that you are not being asked to add private student data or copyrighted material you do not have permission to use.

Generate the materials inventory

AI gets
your content pack, your Teaching Brief, your Pedagogy
AI makes
a numbered inventory. Each item names one material to create, with enough detail to generate it later.
You check
that the list is the right size, covers the brief, and does not include anything the sources cannot support. It is far easier to fix the plan now than to fix many finished documents later.

Generate one teaching material

AI gets
your content pack, your Pedagogy Specification, the matching Teaching Material Specification, and the one inventory item you want to make now.
AI makes
one finished teaching material, written in Markdown, ready to save as a .md file and export with Markdown Mentor.
You check
that the material is accurate, matches the brief and pedagogy, and only uses content from the sources. Edit the Markdown before you export it if you wish.
