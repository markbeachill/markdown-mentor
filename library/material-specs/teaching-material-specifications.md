# Teaching Material Specifications

This file is a catalogue of the kinds of material Markdown Mentor can help you
create. Each entry says what that kind of material must include. The point is
to keep the AI consistent: every worksheet should look like a worksheet, every
quiz like a quiz.

When you ask the AI to plan or generate materials, give it this file so it
follows these shapes. You can add your own material types in the same style.

---

## Slide overview

A short set of slides that introduce a topic.

Must include:
- A title slide with the topic and audience.
- One idea per slide.
- Short lines, not full paragraphs.
- A final slide listing the sources used.

## Student worksheet

A handout a student works through on their own or in class.

Must include:
- A clear title and a one-line purpose.
- A short explanation of the idea.
- At least one worked example.
- Practice tasks that get harder in small steps.
- Space or prompts for the student's own answers.

## Teacher note

A short guide for the person teaching the material.

Must include:
- The learning aim in one sentence.
- The key points to stress.
- Common mistakes to watch for.
- Suggested timings.

## Question-and-answer set

A set of questions with answers, for practice or revision.

Must include:
- Clear questions.
- A correct answer for each question.
- A short explanation for each answer.
- A difficulty label (easy, medium, hard).
- A note of which source each question comes from.

## Revision quiz

A quick test to check memory and understanding.

Must include:
- Ten or fewer questions.
- A mix of recall and understanding.
- An answer key at the end.

## Glossary

A list of key terms and plain-English meanings.

Must include:
- Each term in bold.
- A one-sentence meaning a beginner can understand.
- The terms in alphabetical order.

## Answer key

The answers to a worksheet or quiz, kept separate so it can be withheld from
students.

Must include:
- The question number or text.
- The correct answer.
- A short reason where it helps.

## Lab or practical sheet

Step-by-step instructions for a hands-on task, experiment, or exercise.

Must include:
- The aim of the task in one sentence.
- Anything needed before starting (materials, setup, safety points).
- Numbered steps in the right order.
- What a finished result should look like.
- One or two questions to check understanding afterwards.

## Mark scheme

A guide to marking student work fairly and consistently.

Must include:
- The question or task being marked.
- The points or marks available.
- What earns each mark, in plain terms.
- Example answers at different levels where it helps.

## Rubric

A grid that describes levels of quality for a piece of work.

Must include:
- The things being judged (rows), such as argument, evidence, or accuracy.
- The levels of quality (columns), such as developing, secure, strong.
- A short, plain description in each cell so a learner knows what each level
  looks like.
