# Teaching Brief: Thesis statements for sixth-form students

## What should be taught?

How to write a clear, arguable thesis statement for a short argumentative
essay.

## Who is it for?

Sixth-form students (ages 16 to 18) in their first term of essay writing.

## What level is required?

Beginner to intermediate. Students can write paragraphs but struggle to state
a single clear argument.

## What is the purpose?

By the end, a student should be able to turn a topic into a specific, arguable
thesis and explain why it is stronger than a vague version.

## What should be included?

- A short, plain explanation of what a thesis statement is.
- The difference between a weak and a strong thesis.
- At least one worked example.
- Practice that asks students to rewrite weak theses.

## What should be excluded?

- Full essay structure beyond the thesis.
- Referencing and citation styles.
- Anything above first-term difficulty.
