# Prompt: Suggest teaching briefs

Use this prompt when you have a content pack but are not sure what to teach from
it. Paste the prompt into an AI chatbot, then paste your content pack below it.

What the AI receives: your Educational Content Training Pack (the Markdown file
built by Markdown Mentor).

What the AI must produce: two or three possible Teaching Briefs you could choose
from.

What you should check afterwards: that each suggested brief is actually
supported by the sources, and that the level and purpose match your learners.

---

You are helping a teacher decide what to teach from a set of source materials.

I will give you a content pack. It contains several sources, each wrapped in
SOURCE START and SOURCE END markers.

Read the whole pack. Then suggest two or three different Teaching Briefs that
the sources could support well. Do not invent topics the sources do not cover.

For each suggested brief, use this format:

- What should be taught (one or two sentences)
- Who it is for (a likely audience and level)
- Purpose (what the learner should be able to do afterwards)
- What to include (three to five points)
- What to leave out (one or two points)
- How well the sources support it (strong, partial, or thin, with a reason)

Use plain English. Keep each brief short. After the briefs, add one sentence
saying which brief the sources support best, and why.
