# Prompt: Improve the content pack

Use this prompt after you build a content pack and run the readiness check, if
you want help deciding what sources to add. It does not change your files. It
gives you advice you can act on.

What the AI receives: your content pack, your teaching goal, and (if you have
it) the readiness note from Markdown Mentor.

What the AI must produce: a short list of what is missing or weak, and what kind
of source would fix each gap.

What you should check afterwards: that the suggestions are realistic for you to
find or write, and that you are not being asked to add private student data or
copyrighted material you do not have permission to use.

---

You are reviewing a set of teaching sources to help a teacher strengthen them.

I will give you a teaching goal and a content pack. I may also give you a
readiness note that lists possible problems.

Read everything. Then tell me, in plain English:

1. What the pack covers well for this goal.
2. What is missing or weak (for example: no worked examples, no practice
   questions, no assessment criteria, only background reading, unclear level).
3. For each gap, what kind of source would fix it. Be specific, but suggest the
   type of source, not invented facts.

Do not write the missing material. Do not invent content. Only advise on what
to collect or create. Do not suggest adding private personal data or material
the teacher may not have permission to use.

End with one short paragraph: can the pack support the goal as it stands, or
should more be added first?
