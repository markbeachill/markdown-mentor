# What is a thesis statement?

A thesis statement is one sentence that states the main argument of an essay.
It tells the reader what you will argue and why it matters. A strong thesis is
specific, arguable, and placed near the end of the introduction.

A weak thesis is vague or simply states a fact. A strong thesis takes a
position that a reasonable person could disagree with.

# Worked example: turning a topic into a thesis

Topic: school uniforms.

Weak: "This essay is about school uniforms."
Better: "Schools should require uniforms."
Strong: "Schools should require uniforms because they reduce daily decision
stress and narrow visible class differences between students."

For instance, the strong version names two reasons, so the essay now has a
clear structure to follow.
