# Pedagogy Specification: School level

A Pedagogy Specification explains *how* the material should be taught. The
Teaching Brief says what to teach; this says the teaching style. Choose one
specification and edit it to match your learners.

## Learner type

School students in a classroom, usually with a teacher present.

## Level

From beginner to intermediate. Assume limited prior knowledge.

## Tone

Warm, clear, and encouraging. Plain words. Short sentences.

## Amount of scaffolding

High. Break ideas into small steps. Give a worked example before asking
students to try one themselves.

## Practice style

Frequent, short practice. Move from guided practice (lots of support) to
independent practice (little support).

## Feedback style

Quick and specific. Tell the student what was good and the one thing to change
next.

## Assessment style

Short retrieval questions at the start of a task and a brief check at the end.
Use success criteria the student can understand.

## Broad teaching approach

Explain, model, practise, check. Keep activities short so attention holds.
