# Pedagogy Specification: ESL / EFL

A Pedagogy Specification explains *how* the material should be taught. This one
is for English as a Second or Foreign Language. ESL means English as a Second
Language; EFL means English as a Foreign Language. Edit it to match your
learners.

## Learner type

Adults or teenagers learning English. They may understand the topic in their
own language but find the English hard.

## Level

State the level plainly, for example A2 (basic) or B1 (intermediate) on the
CEFR scale. CEFR is a common European scale for language level.

## Tone

Friendly and clear. Use graded language, which means language kept at or just
above the learner's level.

## Amount of scaffolding

High. Pre-teach key words. Give sentence starters and models.

## Practice style

Move from controlled practice (fill in the gap, match the pairs) to freer
practice (write your own sentence, speak about your own life). Recycle new
vocabulary across several tasks.

## Feedback style

Gentle correction. Focus on meaning first, then on the one or two language
points the lesson is about.

## Assessment style

Short checks that a learner can complete in English at their level. Avoid long
written instructions.

## Broad teaching approach

Present, practise, produce. Build from words to sentences to short
communication. Keep instructions short and supported by examples.
