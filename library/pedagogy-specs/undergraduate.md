# Pedagogy Specification: Undergraduate

A Pedagogy Specification explains *how* the material should be taught. This one
is for university students. Edit it to match your course.

## Learner type

Undergraduate students who can read independently and are building academic
skills.

## Level

Intermediate to advanced. Assume some prior study of the subject.

## Tone

Academic but clear. Treat students as capable adults. Define technical terms
the first time they appear.

## Amount of scaffolding

Moderate. Give structure and signposting, but expect students to do the
reading and thinking themselves.

## Practice style

Discussion questions, evidence evaluation, and applied problems. Favour tasks
with more than one defensible answer.

## Feedback style

Reasoned feedback that points to the argument, the evidence, and the
structure, not just the conclusion.

## Assessment style

Essay questions, seminar contributions, and applied problems. Use criteria that
reward argument and evidence, not recall alone.

## Broad teaching approach

Read, discuss, argue, apply. Build independent thinking and the habit of
backing claims with evidence.
